Passive Scrolling On Social Media Linked To Anxiety, Stress, and Depression

By Aayushi Kapoor | July 26th, 2023

Recent research published by the “Behavior & Information Technology” says that passive scrolling on social media is linked to anxiety, stress, and depression. Are you also someone who endlessly scrolls on social media without even connecting through the content you watch?

Well, I get that. There are some days when we just don’t want to connect with anyone and mindlessly watch reels or posts. But is that the right thing to do? Research suggests passive scrolling on social media can increase the risk of anxiety, depression, stress, and other mental health issues!

Taking a Brief Look at the Study

The Journal of Behavior and Information Technology recently published a study surveying 288 people between the ages of 18 and 34 to explore the link between social media use, psychological distress, and loneliness. The study categorized social media users into three groups: passive scrollers, active non-social media users, and active social media users.

The researchers found that passive scrolling on social media is associated with a higher risk of depression, stress, and anxiety. In contrast, active engagement, such as creating and sharing content, was linked to improved social well-being and reduced distress.

How to Stop Passive Scrolling on Social Media?

  • Acknowledge your feelings and remove triggers: Identify what triggers your mindless scrolling and work on eliminating them to promote mindfulness.
  • Keep track of your scrolling time: Regain control by engaging in hobbies or productivity activities that promote mindfulness.
  • Try to positively engage: Reacting to posts or encouraging others can make you feel more connected and happier.
  • Remove useless information: Engage with content that fascinates you and brings positivity to your social media experience.
  • Observe your surroundings: Take breaks, connect with family, or indulge in mindful activities like playing with a pet.
  • Set limits: Monitor your screen time to avoid mindlessly spending hours on social media.
