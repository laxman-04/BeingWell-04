Depression And Disability: Can Depression Disable An Individual?

By Aayushi Kapoor | October 17th, 2024 | Wellness Guide

A lot of people ask, “Is depression a disability?” To understand this, let's explore what psychotherapists have to say. Severe or chronic depression can impact daily life, making it difficult to work, walk, or even get out of bed. Depression manifests differently in everyone and can sometimes lead to disability.

According to the Americans with Disability Act (ADA), depression is considered a psychiatric disability as it can affect quality of life and, in some cases, the ability to work.

Is Depression a Disability?

Clinical depression is one of the most common mental health disorders worldwide. Here are some common symptoms of depression:

  • Anxiety
  • Changes in appetite
  • Consistent sadness
  • Emptiness
  • Hopelessness
  • Fatigue
  • Irritability
  • Lack of concentration
  • Unexplained weight loss or gain
  • Self-harm or suicidal thoughts

For a diagnosis, these symptoms must persist for more than two weeks. Depression is considered a leading cause of disability in people aged 15 to 44. It often develops after the age of 32, but can occur at any stage of life.

What We Can Do?

Since depression is considered a disability, individuals may qualify for assistance, including:

  1. Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI): For individuals unable to work for 12 months due to depression.
  2. Supplemental Security Income (SSI): For individuals with little assets or low income.
  3. Medicaid: For low-cost healthcare access after two years of SSDI.
  4. ADA Accommodations: Offers reasonable accommodations at work like flexible schedules, mental health breaks, and work-from-home options.

What Happens If I Don’t Qualify for Benefits?

If you don't qualify for disability benefits, you can appeal to the Social Security Administration (SSA) or request accommodations from your workplace by submitting supporting documents.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Is depression classed as disabled? Yes, depression is considered a disability, along with other mental health conditions like bipolar disorder and dementia.
  • Is anxiety a disability? Yes, anxiety disorders such as PTSD, OCD, and panic disorders are considered disabilities.
  • Can you get money for depression? If you are diagnosed with depression and unable to work, you may be eligible for welfare benefits.
  • Is stress a disability? Long-term stress is considered a mental impairment, not a disability.
