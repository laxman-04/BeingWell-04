Smoking & Depression: Dealing With Depression Related To Quitting Smoking

By Aayushi Kapoor | September 1st, 2024 | Depression

A recently published study shows that regular smokers are prone to developing mental health issues such as depression, anxiety, and stress. Nicotine is a highly addictive substance, and people who quit smoking often experience nicotine withdrawal symptoms like irritability, insomnia, restlessness, and depression. Depression is one of the most common complaints during early cessation.

Smoking and Depression: What’s the Connection?

Nicotine increases dopamine in the brain, creating dependence. When smokers quit, they often experience withdrawal, leading to temporary depression. According to psychologists, feeling sad or depressed after quitting is common and typically resolves within days or weeks. However, severe depression may last longer and require professional intervention.

People with a history of mental health issues are at a higher risk of experiencing depression after quitting smoking. Studies show that many experience symptoms of major depressive disorder after cessation.

Common Symptoms of Depression after Quitting Smoking:

  • Anxiety
  • Appetite changes
  • Lack of concentration
  • Irritability
  • Fatigue
  • Sadness
  • Loss of interest
  • Insomnia

How to Deal with Depression after Quitting Smoking?

  1. Seek support from loved ones: Inform your loved ones before quitting and seek their support during tough times.
  2. Learn about your feelings: Recognize and track your symptoms to better understand your emotional needs.
  3. Understand your trigger points: Identify and avoid emotional or social triggers that might cause depressive episodes.
  4. Keep your mind and body moving: Regular physical activity can help alleviate fatigue and depressive symptoms.
  5. Practice deep relaxation exercises: Mindfulness and relaxation exercises can help calm intense cravings and negative thoughts.
  6. Try journaling: Writing down your thoughts can provide an emotional release and help manage depressive feelings.
  7. Connect with a mental health professional: For severe depression, seek professional help for diagnosis and treatment.

It’s important to seek the right treatment and diagnosis when dealing with depression after quitting smoking to ensure better recovery.
